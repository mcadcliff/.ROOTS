The banana's scientific name is musa sapientum, and means “fruit of the wise men.”

8000 BC 500 BC 200 AD 650 AD 1399 1800 1900

Whilst there is archaeological evidence of banana cultivation in New Guinea dating as far back as 8000 BC, other banana domestication projects have been found in Southeast Asia in the jungles of Malaysia, Indonesia and the Philippines.

Early written references to the banana were discovered in Sanskrit dating back to the year 500 BC.

By 200 AD bananas had spread to China. According to the Chinese historian Yang Fu, bananas only ever grew in the southern region of China.

Bananas as we know them began to be developed in Africa about 650 AD.

Bananas started to be traded internationally by the end of 14th century.

Banana mass production exploded across the globe in the late 1880's.

In the 1900s, Cavendish became one of the world’s most popular banana varieties and remains so today.
