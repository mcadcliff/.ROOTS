The more technical name for the big green stalk, maize, came to English from the Spanish maiz, a barely altered version of the Taino (the language native to Hispaniola, the island of Haiti and the Dominican Republic) word for the plant, mahiz.

8000 BC to 7000 BC 1018

Maize was genetically most similar to a teosinte type from the tropical Central Balsas River Valley of southern Mexico, with domestication occurring 9,000 years ago.

From Mexico, maize spread north into the Southwestern United States and south down the coast to Peru around 1018.
