The word mushroom is likely derived from the French word 'mousse' meaning 'moss'.

900 million to 1 billion years ago 200 AD. 600 1600-1699 1865

A multicellular fungus named Ourasphaira giraldae, lived roughly 900 million to 1 billion years ago.

Auricularia polytricha, “ear fungus”, was first cultivated in ancient China around 300 to 200 B.C.

Aztecs and Mazatecs used mushrooms for ritual use as early as 200 AD.

Fungi were most likely cultivated for the first time around the year 600 in Asia.

In Europe, the first cultivated fungi, the mushroom, was introduced in the 17th century.

Mushroom cultivation reached the United States in the late 1800's with imported spores from Mexico.
